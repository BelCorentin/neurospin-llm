====== Getting Started at NeuroSpin ======

Welcome to NeuroSpin, the neuroimaging research centre at CEA Saclay. This page is the main entry point for new members (PhD students, postdocs, engineers, and visiting researchers).

===== First Steps =====

==== Badging and Building Access ====

Your building access badge is issued by the HR office (bâtiment 145, ground floor). You will need:
  * A signed contract or invitation letter
  * A valid photo ID
  * A passport-format photograph

Badge activation takes 1–2 business days. Contact Sylvie Bourdier (sylvie.bourdier@cea.fr) if you have not received your badge within 3 days of arriving.

The MRI scanner rooms (rooms 106 and 108) require an additional RFID clearance granted by the MRI safety officer. See the [[mri_protocols|MRI Protocols]] page for details.

==== IT Account Setup ====

To get a NeuroSpin IT account, fill in the account request form on the intranet at http://intranet.neurospin.fr/it-request. You will need the signature of your direct supervisor.

Your account gives you access to:
  * Email (firstname.lastname@neurospin.fr)
  * The internal wiki (this site)
  * VPN access (required for remote work — see [[network_access|Network Access]])
  * The shared HPC cluster (see below)

Account creation typically takes 24 hours. Contact it-support@neurospin.fr for urgent requests.

==== HPC Cluster Access ====

NeuroSpin operates its own HPC cluster (`nscluster`). To get an account:

  - Email it-support@neurospin.fr with subject "New cluster account request"
  - Include your supervisor's name and your expected data storage needs
  - You will receive credentials and an SSH key within 2 business days

Your home directory on the cluster is at ''/home/$USER'' with a 100 GB quota. Project data should go in ''/data/projects/$PROJECT_NAME'' (see [[data_storage|Data Storage]] for quotas and policies).

===== Mandatory Training =====

All new members must complete the following before starting any research:

  - **MRI Safety Training** (mandatory for anyone who enters the scanner room): book via the intranet calendar under "Formations MRI". Duration: half a day. Run monthly.
  - **Data Protection (RGPD/GDPR)**: online module available on the CEA e-learning platform. Takes approximately 1 hour. Certificate must be sent to hr@neurospin.fr.
  - **Fire Safety**: classroom session, scheduled every 6 months. Check the intranet for next session date.

===== Computing Environment =====

NeuroSpin provides two computing environments:

== Workstations ==
Linux (Ubuntu 22.04) workstations are available in the open-plan office (room 214). Log in with your NeuroSpin credentials. Software available: FSL, FreeSurfer, SPM, AFNI, MRtrix3, Python 3.10 (Anaconda), MATLAB R2023b.

== HPC Cluster ==
For large-scale analyses, use the cluster. It runs SLURM. Standard software modules are available via `module load`. See [[software_install|Software Installation]] for details.

===== FAQ =====

**Q: Can I bring my own laptop?**
A: Yes. Connect it to the NeuroSpin-Guests Wi-Fi network. For access to internal resources (cluster, shared storage), use VPN. See [[network_access|Network Access]].

**Q: How do I access the scanner?**
A: You need MRI safety clearance, a valid booking in the scanner schedule, and approval from a qualified MRI operator. See [[mri_protocols|MRI Protocols]].

**Q: Where do I store my raw DICOM data?**
A: In the dedicated DICOM archive, accessible via the `neurospin-fetch` tool. See [[data_storage|Data Storage]].

**Q: Is there a printing/scanning service?**
A: Yes. Printer HP-NS-214 is in room 214. Your NeuroSpin login credentials work on it. Maximum print quota: 500 pages/month.
